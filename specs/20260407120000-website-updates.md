# MBCC Website Updates — April 2026

**Date:** 2026-04-07
**Author:** Spec generated via interview with stakeholder
**Status:** Ready for implementation

---

## Overview

Six changes to the MBCC website, ranging from content removal (MBA departure) to new sections (contact, attribution, registration disclosure), logo replacement, and /about page restructuring.

---

## Task 1: Remove Malaysia Buddhist Association (MBA)

### Context

MBA is no longer a member of MBCC. All references to MBA as a **current** member must be removed. However, MBA's **historical** involvement must be preserved — the founding story, timeline, and event records are part of MBCC's history.

### Scope of Removal

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| `src/content/organisations/malaysia-buddhist-association.md` | **Delete** the content file entirely |
| `/organisations/malaysia-buddhist-association` page | Page will 404 (no redirect) |
| Homepage organisations grid | Remove MBA card; display remaining 6 orgs equally (no "Founding Member" badge on any org) |
| `/organisations` listing page | Remove MBA from the listing; 6 orgs displayed equally, no special ordering or badges |
| `OrgCard.astro` component | Remove any "Founding Member" badge/label logic if it exists |
| Content collection schema (`content.config.ts`) | Remove `founding` field from org schema if present, or leave it — no org will use it |
| Navigation & footer links | No changes (MBA was never in nav) |

### What to Preserve (Historical References)

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| About page — founding narrative | **Keep as-is.** MBA is named in the founding story (YB Dato Ong Ka Ting, Dato Fu Ah Kiow, Venerable Seck Chek Huang, MBA designated as founding member, etc.). This is historical record. |
| About page — "7 national organisations" in history text | **Update to "6 national organisations"** — this sentence describes current representation, not a historical moment |
| About page — committee section | **Keep as-is for now.** Venerable Sing Kan (MBA) remains listed as President. A separate committee update will follow later. |
| About page — statistics | **Keep as-is.** Numbers are approximate enough to remain valid. |
| Event content (e.g., National Buddhist Conference 2024) | **Preserve.** Any MBA mentions in events are historical records of what happened. |

### Model Recommendation

**Sonnet** — This is primarily a search-and-remove task with well-defined boundaries. No complex logic or design decisions needed during implementation.

## Task 6: Restructure /about Page

### New Page Structure (Top to Bottom)

The page transitions from a history-first layout to a **mission-first** layout. No tabs — single scrolling page.

```
1. Hero (REWRITTEN — mission-focused)
2. MBCC Role & Purpose (NEW)
3. Executive Committee & Governance (NEW)
4. Interfaith & Values (NEW)
5. Objectives Grid (EXISTING — keep as-is)
6. Statistics Cards (EXISTING — keep as-is)
7. Timeline & Founding Story (EXISTING — keep as-is, MBA history preserved)
8. Committee Member Cards (EXISTING — keep as-is for now)
9. Contact Section (NEW — Task 2)
```

### 1. Hero Section (Rewrite)

**Current hero:**
- Tagline: "About"
- Title: "Our Story & Mission"
- Subtitle: "From the first discussions in 1999 to official registration in 2015, MBCC has been the collective voice of Malaysia's Buddhist community."

**New hero:** Rewrite to lead with MBCC's mission and purpose rather than the founding story. The title and subtitle should reflect that MBCC is the national umbrella body uniting Buddhist organisations, not just a historical account. The founding story is still told — just further down the page.

Suggested direction (implementation may refine):
- Tagline: "About MBCC"
- Title: "Uniting Malaysia's Buddhist Community"
- Subtitle: Something derived from: "The national umbrella body fostering collaboration among diverse Buddhist traditions and representing the collective voice of Malaysian Buddhists."

### 2. MBCC Role & Purpose (New Section)

Source content (first two paragraphs from provided details):

> The Malaysian Buddhist Consultative Council (MBCC) is the national umbrella body uniting six major Buddhist national organisations in Malaysia. Established as a consultative platform, MBCC fosters collaboration among diverse Buddhist traditions and represents the collective voice of the Buddhist community.
>
> MBCC and its respective member organizations play a pivotal role in coordinating nationwide initiatives in Dharma propagation, education, welfare, youth development, and cultural activities. Through its specialised committees and member organisations, MBCC strengthens unity among Malaysian Buddhists while contributing to the nation's social and cultural harmony.

Design: Prose section with the site's existing typography styling. Could use a two-column layout (text + decorative element) or a single centered column. Match the existing about page's prose styling.

### 3. Executive Committee & Governance (New Section)

Source content (third paragraph):

> The Council's Executive Committee (Exco), comprising representatives from its member organisations, provides leadership and guidance in shaping MBCC's policies and programs. Acting as the formal channel of representation, the Exco engages with Federal and State governments as well as international counterparts, ensuring that the perspectives and needs of the Buddhist community are recognised at national, regional, and global levels.

### 4. Interfaith & Values (New Section)

Source content (fourth paragraph):

> By working closely with government agencies, interfaith bodies, and civil society, MBCC continues to uphold Buddhist values while promoting dialogue, mutual respect, and constructive partnership. In doing so, MBCC not only strengthens the Buddhist community but also contributes meaningfully to Malaysia's vision of unity, inclusivity, and shared progress.

### Design Notes

- The new sections (2, 3, 4) should follow the existing page's visual rhythm — alternating background colours (white/cream), consistent spacing, prose typography
- Section headings should be clear but not overly formal
- The new content can be broken into sub-sections with visual elements (icons, subtle dividers) if it helps readability, but don't over-design
- The transition from new mission content → existing objectives/stats → existing history should feel natural

### SEO / Structured Data Updates

Update the JSON-LD Organization schema across the site to include:
- **logo:** URL pointing to the real MBCC logo
- **registration number:** Include as `taxID` or custom property
- **contact:** Add `contactPoint` with Wong Tin Song's details (email, phone)
- **numberOfMembers:** Update from 7 to 6 if referenced
- **member organisations:** Remove MBA from any `memberOf` or `member` arrays

### Model Recommendation

**Opus** — This is the most complex task. It requires careful content integration, design judgment for new sections, hero copywriting, and maintaining visual coherence across a restructured page. The interleaving of new and existing content needs a model that can hold the full page context and make nuanced layout decisions.
