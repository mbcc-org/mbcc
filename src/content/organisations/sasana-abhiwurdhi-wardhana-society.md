The Sasana Abhiwurdhi Wardhana Society (SAWS) is a Theravada Buddhist organisation dedicated to the development and preservation of the Buddhist Sasana (dispensation) in Malaysia. The society takes its name from the Pali term meaning "the development and growth of the Buddha's teaching."

SAWS is closely associated with the Buddhist Maha Vihara in Kuala Lumpur and has been instrumental in supporting the resident monastic community and maintaining the temple as a centre of Buddhist learning and practice.

## Preserving the Sasana

SAWS works to ensure that the authentic teachings of Theravada Buddhism are preserved and transmitted to future generations through education, ordination support, and cultural programmes.

## Key Activities

- Supporting the monastic community (Sangha)
- Maintaining Buddhist temples and meditation centres
- Organising Pali and Dhamma study programmes
- Cultural preservation and Buddhist heritage projects
