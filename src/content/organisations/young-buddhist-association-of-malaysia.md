The Young Buddhist Association of Malaysia (YBAM) was founded in 1970 and has since grown into Malaysia's premier Buddhist youth organisation. YBAM serves as the national coordinating body for Buddhist youth groups across the country, nurturing the next generation of Buddhist leaders.

Though rooted in the Mahayana tradition, YBAM welcomes youth from all Buddhist traditions and promotes a spirit of openness and mutual respect. The association organises a wide range of programmes covering Buddhist education, leadership development, cultural activities, and community service.

## Youth Leadership

YBAM is committed to developing young Buddhist leaders who are grounded in Buddhist values while being equipped to serve contemporary society. Through camps, workshops, and leadership programmes, YBAM cultivates compassion, wisdom, and social responsibility among Malaysian Buddhist youth.

## Key Activities

- National Buddhist youth camps and leadership workshops
- University Buddhist society coordination
- Community service and volunteer programmes
- Buddhist cultural performances and arts festivals
- Inter-religious youth dialogue initiatives
