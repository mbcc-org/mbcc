The Malaysia Buddhist Association (MBA) was founded in 1959 and is the oldest national Buddhist organisation in Malaysia. As the founding member of MBCC, MBA holds the permanent presidency of the council, reflecting its central role in unifying the Buddhist community nationwide.

MBA represents the Mahayana tradition and has been instrumental in promoting Buddhist education, cultural activities, and community welfare programmes across Malaysia. The association oversees hundreds of affiliated temples and centres throughout the country.

## Mission

MBA's mission is to promote the study, practice, and propagation of Buddhism in Malaysia, while fostering unity among Buddhist communities of all traditions.

## Key Activities

- Organising national Buddhist ceremonies and celebrations
- Operating Buddhist educational institutions
- Publishing Buddhist literature and resources
- Coordinating welfare and community service programmes
- Representing the Malaysian Buddhist community in inter-religious dialogue
