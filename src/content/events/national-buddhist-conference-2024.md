The National Buddhist Conference 2024, organised by MBCC, brought together over 300 Buddhist leaders, scholars, and community representatives for two days of dialogue, collaboration, and strategic planning at the Kuala Lumpur Convention Centre.

## Conference Theme

"Buddhism in Modern Malaysia: Challenges and Opportunities" was the overarching theme, addressing how the Buddhist community can navigate contemporary issues while preserving core teachings and values.

## Key Sessions

### Day 1 — Foundations
- **Opening Address** by the MBCC President, highlighting the council's achievements and priorities
- **Keynote:** "The Role of Buddhist Organisations in Contemporary Malaysian Society"
- **Panel Discussion:** Buddhist education in the digital age
- **Breakout Sessions:** Youth engagement, temple management, inter-religious relations

### Day 2 — Action
- **Workshop:** Strategic planning for Buddhist community development
- **Panel Discussion:** Collaboration between MBCC member organisations
- **Plenary Session:** Adopting resolutions and setting priorities for 2025
- **Closing Ceremony** with commitment pledges from all member organisations

## Outcomes

The conference produced several key resolutions, including plans for expanded youth programmes, a digital literacy initiative for temple communities, and strengthened coordination between member organisations on government liaison activities.
