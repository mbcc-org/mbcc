The National Wesak Day Celebration 2025 brought together thousands of Buddhists from across Malaysia in a spirit of unity and devotion. Held at the historic Buddhist Maha Vihara in Kuala Lumpur, the event was organised by MBCC with participation from all seven member organisations.

## Programme Highlights

The celebration began with a multi-tradition morning ceremony featuring monks and representatives from Mahayana, Theravada, and Vajrayana Buddhist organisations. This pan-Buddhist approach reflects MBCC's commitment to unity across all Buddhist traditions.

The afternoon programme included Dhamma talks by prominent Buddhist teachers, cultural performances by Buddhist youth groups from YBAM, and community welfare activities including a blood donation drive and free health screening.

## Candlelight Procession

The highlight of the evening was the grand candlelight procession around the temple grounds, with thousands of devotees carrying candles and chanting in unison. Representatives from MBCC member organisations led the procession, symbolising the collective aspiration of the Malaysian Buddhist community.

## Community Impact

Over 5,000 devotثees and members of the public attended the celebration, making it one of the largest national Wesak events in recent years. The event received coverage from major Malaysian media outlets and was attended by government representatives, reflecting MBCC's role in bridging the Buddhist community with national leadership.
